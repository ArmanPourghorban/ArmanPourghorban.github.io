About

Arman Pourghorban received the B.E. degree in Electrical Engineering from Isfahan University of Technology, Iran, in 2020. He is currently a Ph.D. candidate with the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, Charlotte, NC, USA, and also works as a Controls Engineer at Tesla, Inc. His research interests include cooperative control, multi-agent systems, and differential games.

Selected Projects

  • Target–Defender–Intruder Games — Engagement surfaces, capture analysis, and Markov-chain abstractions for sequential attacker arrivals.
  • Trajectory Optimization for 6-DOF Robots — MPC and dynamics-aware path planning with verification and safety constraints.
  • Flexible Assignment Guard — Centralized defender coordination vs. decentralized fixed-sector guards, with improved capture rates.
