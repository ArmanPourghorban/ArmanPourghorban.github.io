About

I’m Arman Pourghorban, a Controls Engineer at Tesla and a PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering (Control Systems & Robotics). My work spans model-predictive control, dynamics-based trajectory optimization, and game-theoretic target defense for multi-agent systems.

Model Predictive Control Path Planning Multi-Agent Systems Reinforcement Learning Optimization ROS / MATLAB / Python

Selected Projects

  • Target–Defender–Intruder Games — Engagement surfaces, capture analysis, and Markov-chain abstractions for sequential arrivals.
  • Trajectory Optimization for 6-DOF Robots — MPC and dynamics-aware path planning; verification & safety circuits.
  • Flexible Assignment Guard — Centralized defender coordination vs. decentralized fixed-sector guards.

Publications (selected)

  • Target defense against multiple arriving intruders, IEEE Transactions on Robotics, submitted 2024.
  • Target defense in conical environments with sensing limitations, IEEE RA-Letters, submitted 2024.
  • Consensus-based target defense with simultaneous attacker capture, IEEE Control Systems Letters, 2024.
  • Target defense against periodically arriving intruders, ACC 2023. DOI: 10.23919/ACC55779.2023.10156423.
